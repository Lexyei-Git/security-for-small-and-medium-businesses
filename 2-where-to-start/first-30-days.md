# First 30 days: quick-win cyber security actions

This 30-day plan gives non-security analysts a clear set of starting actions to reduce cyber risk in a small or medium business.

## Week 1: Understand what you have

- List your key devices (laptops, desktops, servers, smartphones, tablets).
- List your key systems and applications (email, accounting, CRM, file sharing).
- Identify where important data lives (customer data, financial data, employee data).

## Week 2: Secure accounts and devices

- Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) on email and key business systems where possible.
- Make sure each user has their own account (no shared generic accounts if you can avoid it).
- Require strong passwords or passphrases.

## Week 3: Updates and backups

- Turn on automatic updates for operating systems and key applications when possible.
- Ensure you have at least one backup of critical data, stored separately from the original device (for example, reputable cloud backup or external drive).
- Test that you can restore at least one file from backup.

## Week 4: People and awareness

- Send a short message to staff about common phishing signs and what to do with suspicious emails.
- Define a simple rule: “If you are not sure, do not click and ask IT or management.”
- Agree on who to contact first if there is a suspected incident (for example, IT, manager, or external support).
