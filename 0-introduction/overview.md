# Overview

This guide exists to give small and medium businesses a clear, practical starting point for information security.

Many organizations do not have a dedicated security team. Instead, IT generalists, managers or business analysts are asked to “take care of security” on top of their normal responsibilities. This repository is written for those people.

The focus is on:

- Clear language instead of security jargon  
- Concrete actions instead of abstract theory  
- Priority and sequence (what to do first, what can wait)

---

## Scope

The guide focuses on basic cyber security hygiene and realistic controls for small and medium businesses, especially in Canada. It is not a full implementation manual for every regulation or framework.

Topics include:

- Understanding what information security is and why it matters  
- Identifying what assets and data you have  
- Applying basic technical and administrative controls  
- Using recognized frameworks (such as the NIST Cybersecurity Framework and CIS Critical Security Controls) as references, but in simplified form  
- Establishing simple policies and governance suitable for smaller organizations

---

## How the guide is organized

The repository is organized into folders that represent different stages of understanding and implementation:

- `0-introduction/`  
  - High‑level overview of the guide and a “how to use this guide” companion.

- `1-foundations/`  
  - Core concepts and principles (for example, what information security is, CIA triad, least privilege, defense in depth).

- `2-where-to-start/`  
  - First steps and a 30‑day action plan, including simple asset inventory, basic risk assessment and starter policies.

- `3-practical-controls/`  
  - Individual controls such as backups, patching, access control, phishing awareness, incident response, cloud/SaaS security and more.

- `4-frameworks-explained/`  
  - How well‑known frameworks (for example, NIST CSF, CIS Controls) map to small and medium businesses in practical terms.

- `5-maturity-roadmaps/`  
  - Suggested 30‑day, 3–6 month and 12‑month plans for improving security over time.

- `policies/`  
  - Starter policy templates (acceptable use, passwords and MFA, backups, email and phishing, incident reporting, vendor security) and guidance for managers on how to adapt and approve them.

You do not need to read everything in order. However, if you are completely new to security, it is recommended to start with `1-foundations/` and then move to `2-where-to-start/`.  
Managers and decision‑makers may prefer to begin with `0-introduction/how-to-use-this-guide.md` and the roadmaps in `5-maturity-roadmaps/`.

---

## Who should use this guide

This guide is intended for:

- IT generalists and system administrators in small and medium businesses  
- Business professionals (risk, compliance, finance, HR, operations) with security responsibilities  
- Managers who need to understand what “basic security” should look like in their organization

If you are already an experienced security professional, you may still find this useful as a reference when explaining concepts to non‑technical stakeholders or when designing lightweight security programs for smaller organizations.
