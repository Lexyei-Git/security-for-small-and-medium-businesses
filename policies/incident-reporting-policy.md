# Incident Reporting Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how employees and other users must report actual or suspected security incidents so that [Company Name] can respond quickly and effectively.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all users with access to [Company Name] systems, networks or data.

## 3. What is a security incident?

A security incident includes, but is not limited to:

- Suspected compromise of an account (for example, unknown logins, password changes not requested by the user).
- Clicking on a suspicious link or opening a suspicious attachment.
- Loss or theft of company devices or media.
- Unauthorized disclosure or suspected exposure of sensitive data.
- Malware or ransomware detections.
- Any other event that could impact the confidentiality, integrity or availability of systems or data.

## 4. Reporting requirements

Users must report suspected or confirmed incidents:

- **As soon as possible** after discovery.
- To: [Primary contact, for example security@company.com or IT helpdesk].
- If the primary contact is unavailable or compromised, to: [Backup contact or manager].

Reports should include, where known:

- What happened and when.
- Which systems, accounts or data are involved.
- Any actions already taken (for example, disconnected from the network).

Users will **not** be punished for reporting honest mistakes. Early reporting is strongly encouraged.

## 5. Responsibilities of the response team

[Contact / Team] will:

- Acknowledge receipt of incident reports.
- Assess severity and decide on initial containment actions.
- Coordinate with internal stakeholders and external partners as needed.
- Document the incident, actions taken and lessons learned.

## 6. Review

This policy should be reviewed at least annually and after significant incidents.
