# Passwords and Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how passwords and multi-factor authentication (MFA) must be used to protect [Company Name] accounts and systems from unauthorized access.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all user accounts that provide access to [Company Name] systems, networks, applications and data, including cloud services and remote access solutions.

## 3. Password requirements

- Passwords for company accounts must:
  - Be at least **12 characters** long.
  - Preferably be **passphrases** (for example, several words combined).
  - Be **unique** and not reused across different services, especially between personal and work accounts.

- Users must not:
  - Share passwords with any other person, including colleagues and managers.
  - Send passwords by email, chat or other insecure channels.
  - Store passwords in plain text (for example, documents, notes, unencrypted files).

## 4. Password managers

- [Company Name] will provide or approve a **password manager** for storing and sharing passwords securely when necessary.
- Users are encouraged to use the approved password manager instead of trying to memorize complex passwords.

## 5. Multi-factor authentication (MFA)

- MFA is **required**, where technically possible, for at least:
  - Company email accounts and identity platforms.
  - Administrator and privileged accounts.
  - Remote access (VPN, remote desktop gateways, management tools).
  - Systems that handle sensitive or regulated data.

- Approved MFA methods include:
  - Authenticator apps (recommended).
  - Hardware security keys for high-risk or privileged users.
  - SMS or phone calls only where no better option is available, and with additional awareness.

- Users must:
  - Not approve MFA prompts they did not initiate.
  - Report unexpected or suspicious MFA prompts immediately to [Contact / Team].

## 6. Account management

- Accounts must be created, modified and disabled according to documented joiner–mover–leaver processes.
- When a user leaves [Company Name] or no longer needs access, their accounts must be promptly disabled or removed.
- Shared or generic accounts should be avoided and, where necessary, strictly controlled and protected.

## 7. Review

This policy should be reviewed at least annually or whenever major changes occur in authentication technologies or security requirements.
