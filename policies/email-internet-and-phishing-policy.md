# Email, Internet and Phishing Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy sets expectations for the safe use of email and internet services at [Company Name] and describes how users should handle phishing attempts and other suspicious messages.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all users who access [Company Name] email, messaging and internet services from any device.

## 3. Email use

Users must:

- Use company email accounts for business communications with customers, partners and suppliers.
- Be cautious when opening attachments or clicking links, especially in unexpected or unsolicited messages.
- Verify unusual requests (for example, urgent payment changes, requests for sensitive data) via a trusted channel before acting.

Users must not:

- Use company email to send offensive, discriminatory or illegal content.
- Automatically forward company email to personal accounts without approval.
- Use personal email accounts for business-critical communication unless explicitly approved.

## 4. Phishing and suspicious messages

Users must:

- Treat messages with urgent or unusual requests, unexpected attachments or unknown senders as suspicious until verified.
- Not click on links or open attachments in suspicious messages.
- Report suspected phishing emails to [Contact / Team], using the agreed method (for example, “Report phishing” button, forwarding to a dedicated mailbox).

[Company Name] will:

- Implement email security controls (for example, spam filters, malware scanning, external sender tags).
- Provide periodic training and examples of common phishing tactics.

## 5. Internet use

- Internet use on company devices must be primarily for business purposes.
- Access to clearly inappropriate or high-risk sites (for example, illegal content, untrusted download sites) is prohibited.
- Content filtering or blocking may be used to reduce risk.

## 6. Public and home networks

- When using public Wi‑Fi, users should connect through approved secure methods (for example, VPN) before accessing sensitive systems.
- Users are encouraged to secure home Wi‑Fi with strong passwords and modern encryption (for example, WPA2/WPA3).

## 7. Review

This policy should be reviewed at least annually, or after significant phishing or email-related incidents.
